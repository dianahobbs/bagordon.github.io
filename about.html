About BAGL

Lab Mission

The Brian A. Gordon Lab (BAGL) focuses on understanding the complexities of the aging brain. This includes studies of both healthy aging as well as neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer disease. We integrate cognitive testing alongside advanced neuroimaging techniques, including MRI (volumetric, DTI, and fMRI) and PET imaging (amyloid, tau, and FDG). Our research crosses multiple domains and is at the intersection of cognitive neuroscience, psychology, neurology and radiology.


Lab Philosophy

Our lab is guided by a strong set of values and a clear philosophy. We believe in fostering a collaborative and inclusive environment where everyone's contributions are valued and respected. We promote open communication, teamwork, and the exchange of ideas. We prioritize integrity, ethical conduct, and responsible use of resources and data. Innovation, curiosity, and a commitment to excellence are at the core of our lab's philosophy. We strive to make a positive impact on scientific research and the well-being of individuals through our work.


Code of Conduct

We strive to create an inclusive, collaborative, and respectful environment where all members can thrive. We embrace diverse perspectives and foster an atmosphere of open communication. We value integrity and uphold ethical standards for the responsible use of resources and data. Harassment, discrimination, and any form of misconduct will not be tolerated. We work together as a team, supporting and uplifting one another, and recognizing the contributions of all members. By adhering to this code of conduct, we create a positive and enriching experience for everyone in our lab and university community.
