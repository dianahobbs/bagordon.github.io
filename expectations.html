Lab Expectations


General Expectations

Individual Mentoring Plans: We prioritize individual mentoring plans to support the professional growth and development of each member. These personalized plans are designed to identify and address specific career goals, skill-building opportunities, and areas for improvement. Through regular 1-on-1 meetings, we work closely with our lab members to provide guidance, mentorship, and resources tailored to their unique needs. Our goal is to foster a supportive and collaborative environment that empowers individuals to reach their full potential and achieve their career aspirations.

Working Hours: Our lab recognizes the importance of a flexible work environment to accommodate the diverse needs of graduate and postdoctoral trainees. While we don't enforce strict working hours, we do value regular attendance and effective communication. As a member of this lab, you have the flexibility to manage your schedule as long as you fulfill your commitments, meet project deadlines, and maintain open lines of communication with your supervisor and team. We trust that you will prioritize your responsibilities and ensure your work contributes to the overall success of the lab. Together, we can strike a balance between productivity and personal well-being.

Email: We encourage lab members to maintain effective and timely email communication. We appreciate your commitment to maintaining open lines of communication through email, contributing to a collaborative and productive work environment.

Attendance: Attendance is crucial for active engagement and participation in various lab activities and events. As a member of our lab, you are expected to attend seminars, weekly lab meetings, 1-on-1 meetings, start and end of semester events, as well as the annual graduate research symposium. These gatherings provide valuable opportunities for knowledge exchange, scientific discussions, networking, and showcasing your research. By attending these events, you contribute to the vibrant and collaborative atmosphere of our lab, while also enhancing your own professional development and building connections within the scientific community.

Outreach: Outreach is an essential aspect of our lab's mission. We believe in the importance of sharing our scientific discoveries and engaging with the broader community. Through varioius outreach activities, such as public lectures, science fairs, and community events, we aim to communicate our research findings in an accessible and engaging manner. By fostering dialogue and promoting scientific literacy, we strive to inspire curiosity, spark interest, and create a positive impact beyond the walls of our lab. We are dedicated to fostering a culture of outreach and encourage all lab memebers to actively participate in these initiatives, contributing to the broader dissemination of knowledge and the advancement of scientific understanding.

Publications: Publications and authorship are fundamental components of our lab's scientific endeavors. We value the dissemination of our research findings through high-quality publications in peer-reviewed journals. As lab members, you will have the opportunity to contribute to the development and writing of manuscripts, ensuring that your intellectual contributions are recognized and acknowledged. Authorship is determined based on the established criteria of substantial intellectual contribution and active participation in the research process. We adhere to ethical standards and guidelines for authorship, promoting fairness, transparency, and accountability. Our lab encourages collaborative and interdisciplinary research, fostering opportunities for co-authorship with colleagues within and outside the lab. By actively engaging in the publication process, we aim to advance scientific knowledge, enhance our professional reputation, and contribute to the scientific community at large.

Graduate Students: As a member of our lab, it is essential that you take ownership of your degree and academic journey. This entails staying proactive and well-informed about program and university deadlines, requirements, and any other pertinent information. By remaining organized and attentive to these responsibilities, you can ensure a smooth and successful progression towards your degree. We are here to support and guide you along the way, but ultimately, the responsibility for staying on top of your academic obligations lies with you. Together, we can create a productive and rewarding experience during your time in our lab.

Postdoctoral Research Associates: As a postdoctoral researcher in our lab, it is crucial that you take charge of your professional development and career advancement. You are responsible for staying informed about relevant opportunities, deadlines, and requirements that are essential for your success. This includes keeping track of funding opportunities, publication deadlines, conference presentations, and any other professional commitments. By actively engaging in these aspects of your career, you can maximize your potential for growth and make significant contributions to your field. While we are here to provide guidance and support, the responsibility for managing your postdoctoral journey lies with you. Together, we can create a stimulating and impactful research environment that fosters your professional growth and success.

Brain's Commitment: As part of the lab, you can expect to receive comprehensive support from Brian. This includes technical assistance for data analysis, along with valueable feedback and constructive criticism of your work. In addition, Brian is committed to providing professional career support, helpinging you navigate both the technical and non-technical aspects of advancing your career. Regular meetings will be held every week to discuss your work, address any challenges, and ensure progress towards your goals. Furthermore, Brian will provide transparent and honest appraisals of your funding situation, allowing you to plan effectively and make informed decisions. With these resources and ongoiong support, you will have the necessary tools to excel in your research and professional development.

How We Give Feedback

Most of the feedback we give and receive is when giving and attending practice talks. We expect feedback to be supportive, but constructive.

Individual feedback will be given on a 1, 5, and 12 month basis for new students and hires. After your first year, feedback is given on an annual basis. Progress report documents can be found on WUSTL Box under "Gordon Lab Folder > Onboarding Information > Check-In Resources".


Research Opportunities

We actively contribute to the scientific community by sharing our research findings through local, national, and international conferences, as well as publishing in esteemed scientific journals.

Funding is an essential aspect of our lab. Brian typically ensures that students joining the lab are guaranteed a minimum of five years of full funding. We strongly encourage trainees to explore and apply for external funding opportunities to support their work. These opportunities encompass scholarships, fellowships, and grants that can cover salaries, as well as smaller grants for travel, workshop attendance, and research expenses. For additional resources on funding, please refer to our comprehensive list of available resources.


Code of Conduct

We strive to create an inclusive, collaborative, and respectful environment where all members can thrive. We embrace diverse perspectives and foster an atmosphere of open communication. We value integrity and uphold ethical standards for the responsible use of resources and data. Harassment, discrimination, and any form of misconduct will not be tolerated. We work together as a team, supporting and uplifting one another, and recognizing the contributions of all members. By adhering to this code of conduct, we create a positive and enriching experience for everyone in our lab and university community.
